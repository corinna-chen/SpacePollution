Mars

Portfolio

Temperature -87 to -5 degrees celsius
Days in a year 687 days

We have all heard the, "there was once life on Mars," argument but is it true? Nope, you were lied to (I know it hurts â˜¹ ). There is no proof of past or present life on Mars. There is evidence suggesting that the surface environment of Mars had liquid water and may have been habitable for microorganisms during the ancient Noachian period. While some microbes from Earth can survive on Mars, it doesn't suggest humans can. The radiation, reduced air pressure, and an atmosphere with only 0.16% oxygen makes it close to impossible to live on Mars. Space pollution also poses an issue on Mars! Eight out of 14 orbiting objects in Martian orbit are inactive, which makes more than half of the population around Mars space pollution. Additionally, as rovers and more technology land on Mars, more space pollution will live on Mars. Space pollution on Mars could worsen to the point where it would be difficult to enter or exit the planet without colliding with space pollution.
